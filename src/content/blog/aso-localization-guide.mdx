# ASO Localization Guide: How to Reach Global App Markets

Your app might be successful in one country, but leaving it unlocalized means missing out on 80% of the global smartphone market. App Store Optimization localization isn't just translation—it's adapting your entire presence to resonate with local audiences.

## The 5 Pillars of ASO Localization

### 1. Screenshot Localization

Screenshots are the most impactful element to localize. Users scroll through them in seconds, and localized text immediately signals relevance.

**What to localize:**
- Headline text on screenshots
- In-app content visible in mockups
- Cultural imagery and context
- Device frames (some markets prefer specific devices)

**Cultural considerations:**

| Market | Visual Preferences |
|--------|-------------------|
| Japan | Dense information, cute aesthetics, detailed explanations |
| US | Clean, minimal, bold statements |
| Germany | Privacy emphasis, detailed features |
| Brazil | Warm colors, personal connections |
| Middle East | Right-to-left layout, family-oriented imagery |

### 2. Metadata Localization

Your app name, subtitle, and description need careful localization—not just translation.

**App Name Strategy:**
- Keep brand name consistent
- Localize the descriptive portion
- Research local keyword volume

**Example:**
| Language | App Name |
|----------|----------|
| English | PhotoEdit - AI Photo Editor |
| German | PhotoEdit - KI Bildbearbeitung |
| Japanese | PhotoEdit - AI写真加工 |
| Spanish | PhotoEdit - Editor de Fotos IA |

### 3. Keyword Localization

Keywords require research in each target language. Direct translation often misses the mark because:

- Search behavior differs by culture
- Slang and colloquialisms vary
- Some concepts don't translate directly

**Keyword Research Process:**
1. Identify your core keywords in English
2. Use local keyword tools (App Annie, Sensor Tower with locale settings)
3. Check competitor keywords in each market
4. Validate with native speakers
5. Monitor and iterate based on performance

### 4. App Description Localization

Your description should be rewritten, not just translated. Consider:

- **Tone:** Formal vs. casual varies by culture
- **Structure:** Some markets prefer bullet points, others prefer narrative
- **Social proof:** Local app awards, local user testimonials
- **Payment methods:** Mention locally relevant payment options

### 5. App Preview Videos

If you use app preview videos, they need localization too:

- Voiceover in local language
- On-screen text translated
- Music that resonates locally
- Scenarios relevant to local users

## Common Localization Mistakes

### 1. Machine Translation Only
Google Translate for ASO metadata is a recipe for disaster. It misses nuance, cultural context, and marketing impact.

### 2. Ignoring Local Competitors
Each market has local competitors. Research what works for them.

### 3. Same Screenshots Everywhere
A screenshot that converts in the US might fail in Japan. Adapt visual density, text length, and imagery.

### 4. Forgetting Right-to-Left Languages
Arabic, Hebrew, and Persian require mirrored layouts. This includes screenshot text and app UI.

### 5. Neglecting Seasonal Differences
Summer in Australia is winter in the US. Holiday-themed content needs local timing.
